Activity Descriptions

  1. Class Learning and Exploration

    This activity happens only once. During it you should read the provided books and resources to gain an understanding of the topics. Make sure you are reading to understand not just get through the book. Since you are new to programming, I'd think you'd need to ask me, your teacher, teammates, and classmates questions to understand what you're reading. You should also help out your mates. “…whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap.” Galations 6:7

    As part of this exploration, you should create all the examples in the book and get them working. Then, you should create examples of your own that illustrate these same principles. These examples will not be graded. To be of most use to you, you should discuss and ask questions about these examples with me, your teammates, and classmates. Use us as resources, but do your own thing.

    Doing your own thing means you haven't duplicated or modified what someone else did. Doing your own thing means you've stretched yourself. That you've applied what you've learned to your own life. Feel free to apply your creativity and make your examples fit something you enjoy doing. What are your hobbies? What do you have some level of expertise in? What do you have some experience with? Those are always good places to start when you're looking for problems you can solve and examples you can create.

  2. Personal Exploration

    This activity happens three times during the semester. For it you'll select three things you haven't yet done personal explorations for. Use your knowledge you gained in the Class Learning and Exploration activity to go deeper and broader in the three things you pick. During this time, Google will be your friend. Use it to search for how to's and other ideas. If you feel you need feedback on the choices you'll make, ask me, your teacher, and I'll be happy to help out. But don't ask me for what things you should explore. Instead, make some decisions and then ask me for ideas on how to make your ideas great.

    Interact with your team. Use them as sounding boards. When you discover something neat, share it. Your ideas can trigger ideas in their heads. Talk. Share. That is most of what goes on when you're exploring. But be careful. Don't fall into the trap of duplicating/slightly modifying what they have found and claiming it as your own. That would be dishonest.

    You'll obviously want to play with what you've discovered. Make new examples or modify the examples you made in the Class Learning and Exploration activity. Have fun. Play around with this stuff. You've got two weeks for each of these activities.

  3. Team Challenge

    This activity also happens three times during the semester. For it you'll come together as a team to overcome the challenge I, your teacher, give you. As a team, design and create a pleasing solution to the challenge.

    Here is the definition you should use.

    Pleasing:
    Easy on the eye and mind. It looks good, the layout work well. It is easy to use and well organized.

    Go wild with these challenges. Think big. This is the time to show what you can do. Bring together all the stuff you've learned so far.

    At the end of each of these challenges you should request to be assessed against the outcomes. Take a look below to see what you should provide the instructor for this assessment.

Being Assessed

Through your continual interactions with me as you do these activities, and by observing your on-going interactions with your team and classmates, I develop a deep understanding of your attainment of the outcomes. This understanding (assessment) is much deeper than a test or quize could give me. And the ongoing feedback you get is also much better than what you could get from a test or quiz. Use class time to get as much interaction with me as you possibly can. That is how you'll learn and grow the best.

You trigger recording an assessment when you submit an assessment recording request.You must have created this document yourself. The document lists each of the three topics you did for your personal exploration and must be written as if it was a letter. You must state you have completed any changes requested by me, your teacher, for each of the individual exploration examples. You must also provide evidence that you contributed significantly to the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript created by your team to meet the challenge this assessment request is tied to.

As part of this letter, you have an oportunity to have your attainment of the first three outcomes assessed. To do this, discuss how you are becoming or what you did to continue becoming outcomes 1, 2, and 3. It your responsibility to explain and express these well. It is not my responsibility to 'pull these out of you.'

As part of the assessemnt request, be the honest person you are. It will hurt you in life, your career, your salvation, and the class if you choose to deceive.

Last of all, sign the letter digitally.

Professional Empathy

When you write this letter, put yourself in my shoes (In programming we call this having professional empathy). Am I going to be able to fully remember and understand what you've done based on your explanation? If not, you may need to be more explicit.

On the other hand, have you been too explicit? Are there things you've included that don't need to be there? If there are, remove them and reword what you've written.
